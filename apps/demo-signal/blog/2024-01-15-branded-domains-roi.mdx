We ran a six-week A/B test across 1.2 million link impressions to measure the impact of branded short domains on click-through rate. The generic links used `go.link/` prefixes. The branded links used the client's own domain. The difference was not subtle.

Branded domains averaged a 34% higher click-through rate across every channel we tested. The effect was strongest in email (41% lift) and weakest in paid social (18% lift), which makes sense — email recipients have more time to evaluate a link before clicking.

## The Test Setup

We partnered with three mid-market SaaS companies and split their link traffic evenly between generic and branded Beacon links. Every other variable stayed constant: same copy, same placement, same audience segments.

| Channel     | Generic CTR | Branded CTR | Lift |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|------|
| Email       | 3.2%        | 4.5%        | 41%  |
| Social      | 1.1%        | 1.4%        | 27%  |
| Paid social | 0.8%        | 0.9%        | 18%  |
| SMS         | 4.6%        | 6.1%        | 33%  |
| In-app      | 2.3%        | 3.2%        | 39%  |

SMS showed the second-highest lift. On a small screen with limited context, a recognizable domain is one of the few trust signals available. Users scanning a text message decide in under a second whether a link is worth tapping.

## Trust Is a Click Multiplier

The mechanism is straightforward. A link that reads `go.acme.com/pricing` carries implicit trust that `go.link/x8f3m` does not. The user recognizes the domain. They associate it with a brand they already interact with. The cognitive cost of clicking drops.

This is not a branding exercise. It is a conversion lever. A 34% CTR lift applied to a million monthly link clicks is 340,000 additional engaged sessions — sessions that would have been lost to a moment of hesitation.

## Setting Up Branded Domains in Beacon

Beacon supports custom branded domains with automatic SSL provisioning:

```json title="Branded domain configuration"
{
  "domain": "go.acme.com",
  "ssl": "auto",
  "fallback": "https://acme.com",
  "attribution": {
    "defaultChannel": "branded",
    "tracePrefix": "acm"
  }
}
```

DNS configuration requires a single CNAME record pointing to the Beacon edge network. SSL certificates are provisioned and rotated automatically. Most teams are live within fifteen minutes of updating their DNS.

## The Compounding Effect

Branded domains also improve link sharing behavior. When a user forwards a `go.acme.com` link to a colleague, the colleague sees a recognizable domain — not a random short URL that looks like spam. This reduces the dark traffic penalty on forwarded links and keeps more of the attribution chain intact.

The ROI case is simple: branded domains cost almost nothing to configure and they improve every metric downstream of the click.
