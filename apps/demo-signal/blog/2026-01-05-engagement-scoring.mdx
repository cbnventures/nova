Lead scoring traditionally requires a CRM, a marketing automation platform, and a six-month implementation project. We wanted to know: how good of an engagement score can you build from nothing but Beacon click data? The answer surprised us. Click-derived engagement scores predicted conversion with 78% accuracy — within 5 percentage points of scores built from full CRM data.

The implication is significant. If you have Beacon links in your campaigns, you already have the raw material for a useful engagement model. You do not need a CRM integration to start.

## The Scoring Model

We built a scoring model using four signals available from Beacon click data:

- **Frequency**: Number of Beacon link clicks in the trailing 30 days.
- **Recency**: Days since last Beacon link click.
- **Depth**: Number of distinct content categories visited (pricing, docs, blog, case studies).
- **Velocity**: Rate of change in click frequency over the trailing 14 days (accelerating, steady, or decelerating).

Each signal is normalized to a 0-100 scale and weighted:

```json title="Engagement score formula"
{
  "model": "click-engagement-v1",
  "weights": {
    "frequency": 0.25,
    "recency": 0.30,
    "depth": 0.25,
    "velocity": 0.20
  },
  "decayFunction": "exponential",
  "halfLife": "14d"
}
```

Recency gets the highest weight because it is the strongest single predictor. A user who clicked yesterday is more likely to convert than a user who clicked thirty times last month but has gone silent.

## Accuracy Comparison

We tested the click-derived score against two baselines across 18 accounts:

| Scoring Method     | Prediction Accuracy | False Positive Rate | Data Sources Required  |
|--------------------|---------------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| CRM + MAP          | 83%                 | 12%                 | CRM, email, web, forms |
| Click-derived only | 78%                 | 16%                 | Beacon clicks only     |
| Demographics only  | 54%                 | 38%                 | Firmographic data      |

The click-derived score is 5 points behind the full CRM model but 24 points ahead of demographics alone. It requires a single data source. For teams without a CRM integration — or teams whose CRM data is incomplete — this is a meaningful upgrade over the alternative.

## Pulse Integration

Engagement scores flow into Pulse dashboards as a real-time metric. When a user's score crosses a threshold, Pulse can trigger an alert:

- Score crosses 80: Notify the sales team that a high-engagement lead is active right now.
- Score drops below 30 after being above 60: Flag the user as at risk of disengaging.
- Score increases by more than 20 points in a single day: A research spike is happening — this user is actively evaluating.

The combination of Lens segmentation and Pulse alerting turns passive click data into an active signal pipeline. You are not waiting for a weekly report to discover that a prospect went from casual browsing to intense evaluation. You find out in minutes.

## Limitations

Click-derived scoring has blind spots. It cannot capture offline interactions, phone calls, or in-app behavior that does not route through Beacon links. It works best for top-of-funnel and mid-funnel engagement where link clicks are the primary interaction.

For teams that need full-funnel scoring, the click-derived model is a starting point — not a replacement. But for teams that have nothing today, 78% accuracy from a single data source is a defensible place to start.
