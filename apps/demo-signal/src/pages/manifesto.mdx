# Manifesto

Signal exists because most data does not deserve a chart. We believe analytics teams spend too much energy producing visualizations and too little energy deciding which numbers earn the screen real estate. A dashboard with twelve metrics is a dashboard with eleven distractions.

## Earning the Screen

Screen real estate is a finite resource. Every visual that lands on a dashboard pushes another one off, or worse, dilutes both. We treat charts the way an editor treats words — anything that is not pulling weight is pulling something out of the way.

## Terminal Density

Small, dense, monospaced. Scannable in two seconds. The dashboard we want looks more like `htop` than like a marketing deck. Density is not a style; it is a service to the reader who has six other tabs open.

## Cutting Through

The metric that needs a tooltip to be understood is a metric that is not yet ready to ship. We send it back. The reader's time is the cost we are spending; we should know what we are buying.

### What a tooltip means

A tooltip is a confession. We hide the confessions while we wait for the metric to mature, then we delete the confessions when the metric earns its place.
