# Manifesto

Foundry exists because how something is made matters as much as what it is. We believe in tolerances over feelings — a three-thousandths-of-an-inch deviation is an objective fact, an opinion is not. Materials have grain. Steps in a process build on each other. Skipping the surface prep does not save time; it relocates the failure to a worse moment.

## Tolerances Over Feelings

A part that fits to three-thousandths is a part that fits. A part that fits "well enough" is a deferred problem. Measurement does not have moods. The caliper does not argue. We choose tolerances when they are inconvenient, because the moment we choose feelings the work begins to drift.

## Surface Prep Is Not Overhead

Every shop has watched a finish coat fail because the surface underneath was not ready. The fix takes longer than the prep would have. The cost is double the budget and the customer remembers. We do the prep. We document the prep. We do not negotiate the prep.

## What Documentation Earns

We document the way the work is actually done, including the parts that look unnecessary on the cut sheet. A clear cut sheet does not produce a better part; it produces a part that can be remade. That is the entire point — every step you cannot reconstruct is a step you cannot improve.

### The unnecessary parts

The steps that look optional are the steps that quietly hold the rest together. They are not waste. They are the load path.
